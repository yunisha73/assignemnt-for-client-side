Safety & Rescue

Stay Safe on the Trail

Explore rescue planning, altitude awareness, and emergency response for Himalayan treks.

Altitude awareness

Identify early signs of AMS and descend quickly to prevent serious complications.

Emergency kit

Keep first aid, communication tools, and warm layers within easy reach on every day hike.

Team communication

Share your route, use radios, and confirm checkpoints with your group before departure.

Rescue planning

Prepare for unexpected events with a clear plan and rescue contacts.

  • Register your trek, know local rescue services, and have a backup way to call for help.
  • Practice basic first aid and use a satellite device when mobile coverage is weak.
  • Keep your team together and follow the guide if conditions worsen.
Helicopter access

Check landing zones and evacuation procedures before starting remote treks.

Weather watch

Monitor forecasts carefully and choose lower camps if storms approach.
